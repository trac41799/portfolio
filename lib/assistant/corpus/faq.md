# Frequently asked questions

## Why did he move from embedded software to AI?
Trac started at Bosch working on automotive Engine Control Units, which gave him rigor around the
full software lifecycle, testing, and safety-critical constraints. He was drawn to deep learning
during his M.S., where research on error-correction coding turned into first-author IEEE
publications. That research foundation now underpins his production AI work — he treats LLM systems
as things to be measured and evaluated, not hoped about.

## What is his strongest technical contribution?
The generative-UI artifact system: rendering live, design-system UI and sandboxed HTML inside chat
responses, backed by a two-stage fast-model-plus-enhancer pipeline with progressive enhancement.
He owned this feature in a production learning-management platform.

## Does he only build from scratch, or can he use frameworks?
Both. He built multi-agent orchestration (router → planner → executor), custom multi-model LLM
clients, and stream-tag interception from first principles in raw Python. He can also express the
same architecture in LangGraph, LangChain, and the Vercel AI SDK.

## What kind of role is he looking for?
Senior AI / full-stack engineering roles building AI-native products — agentic systems, RAG, and
production LLM applications.
