# About Nguyen Dang Trac

Nguyen Dang Trac is an AI Automation Engineer based in Vietnam. He builds AI-native products end
to end: from-scratch multi-agent systems, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) pipelines,
streaming AI user experiences, and multi-tenant SaaS platforms. His daily stack is Next.js,
FastAPI, Supabase (PostgreSQL), and Vercel.

## Career path

Trac's path runs from automotive embedded software into deep-learning research and then into
production AI product engineering.

- **Bosch Global Software Vietnam (Jun 2021 – Aug 2022) — Embedded Software Engineer.** Developed
  and validated automotive embedded software for Engine Control Units (ECU) across the full SDLC.
  Programmed in C, Python, and XML with CAN and FlexRay protocols. Delivered features under Agile
  Scrum and the V-Model, and maintained Jenkins-based CI/CD pipelines.
- **University of Ulsan, South Korea (Sep 2022 – Aug 2024) — Research Assistant.** Led deep-learning
  research on error-correction coding. Published first-author in IEEE Transactions on Cognitive
  Communications and Networking and won the Best Paper Award at ICGHIT 2024. Designed Transformer,
  CNN, and RNN architectures in PyTorch, TensorFlow, and Keras.
- **Edge8 AI (Jan 2025 – present) — AI Automation Engineer.** Architected and built multiple
  multi-tenant SaaS products and internal platforms across AI-native, content, analytics, and
  regulated health-tech domains — around 40 production repositories, all on Supabase and Vercel.

## What he is known for

Trac's most differentiated contribution is a generative-UI artifact system he built for a
production learning-management platform: instead of rendering plain Markdown, the assistant streams
live, design-system-styled UI — cards, tables, and full HTML "artifacts" — directly inside chat
responses. He designed the two-stage pipeline where a fast model emits an artifact specification and
a stronger enhancer model produces polished, sandboxed HTML, delivered with progressive enhancement.

He is comfortable both building agent systems from first principles (raw Python, no framework) and
expressing the same architecture in industry-standard frameworks such as LangGraph.
